## Evidence

### Tile-emission pattern with fixed HUD layout (rank=high)

Every iteration writes 4 s16 fields at offsets `+0x8` (x), `+0xA`
(y), `+0xC` (w), `+0xE` (h) -- the canonical PSX tile/sprite
geometry layout. The coordinates are HUD-typical:

* Section 1 (x stepping 0x6F..0x210 by 0x20 = 17 tiles): a
  horizontal row of small 2x63 tiles at y=50.
* Section 2-3 (3 large tiles at x=0x51): a vertical column of larger
  widgets at the left edge.
* Section 4 (5 tiles with x stepping 0x6A by 0x21, values 5..1): a
  countdown / strength meter.

The "horizontal row of small tiles + vertical column + countdown
meter" is the BB2 **HUD layout** -- the round timer / health bar /
weapon status combination drawn at the top of every gameplay frame.

### Uses `D_800A3580` as the OT/prim-buffer base (rank=medium)

Every helper call takes `D_800A3580` as the first arg. This global
is the **HUD prim-buffer base** -- the same buffer that
[`bb2_rand`](func_80079154.md)'s callers in `text1b.c` reference at
`text1b.c:14164` via `D_800A3580` indirection.

### `func_80072F30` vs `func_80072FCC` (rank=medium)

Two different emit helpers are used: `func_80072F30` for sections
1-3 and `func_80072FCC` for the loops with countdown values. The
two helpers likely differ in what kind of primitive they emit
(opaque tile vs textured sprite, for example). Both are unnamed --
naming them would refine the slug here.
