## Evidence

**Spawns a particle from a hit event**: gets a new particle slot via `coli_hit_body_weapon` (already-named -- collision/hit detector), copies the impact position from `*a1` into the particle's `+0x2C` field, then randomises:

- Velocity X (`+0x44`): signed 8-bit centered at 0 (`rng_Next & 0xFF - 0x80`).
- Velocity Y (`+0x48`): always negative (`-rng_Next() & 0x3F) - 0x80` = upward kick, 0x80..0xBF magnitude).
- Velocity Z (`+0x4C`): signed 8-bit centered at 0.
- Spin (`+0x5C`): random sign + 0x200..0x5FF magnitude.
- Spin axis (`+0x5E`): signed 11-bit centered at 0.

Then pops the front entry of a 16-bit queue at `a0+0x332` (the hit-event queue inside the per-character struct -- offset matches positions used by `func_80032854`).

## Why this name

Spawns a damage / hit particle with randomised velocity (upward Y bias for a 'spark' feel), spin, and consumes one entry from the per-character hit-event queue. Uses the existing rng_Next + coli_hit_body_weapon helpers.
